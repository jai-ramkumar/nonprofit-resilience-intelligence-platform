# Paper Outline: Towards an Early Warning Framework for Nonprofit Organizational Stress Using Public IRS Form 990 Data

---

## Working Title

Towards an Early Warning Framework for Nonprofit Organizational Stress Using Public IRS Form 990 Data

---

## Research Question

Can publicly available IRS Form 990 data provide early indicators of nonprofit organizational stress before those risks become visible through traditional annual reporting?

---

## Research Motivation

Nonprofit organizations often operate under financial uncertainty, funding concentration, rising service demand, and limited reserves. Traditional nonprofit assessments usually focus on historical financial health, but nonprofit leaders need earlier signals that help them understand whether their organization is becoming more resilient or more vulnerable over time.

This paper explores whether public IRS Form 990 filings can be used to develop an early warning framework for nonprofit organizational stress.

The initial focus is not to predict failure with certainty. The emphasis is on supporting earlier and more informed decision making rather than replacing human judgment. The goal is to evaluate whether public financial and organizational indicators can provide useful directional signals for decision support.

---

## Proposed Framework

This paper proposes a Phase 1 scoring framework built around two concepts.

### 1. Financial Resilience Score

A comparative score that measures the financial stability and trajectory of a nonprofit organization using multi-year IRS Form 990 data.

Candidate indicators include:

- Reserve ratio
- Operating margin
- Government funding dependency
- Revenue growth trend
- Expense growth trend
- Program expense ratio

### 2. Capacity Gap Framework

A research framework based on the hypothesis that organizational stress may emerge when community need grows faster than organizational capacity.

In Phase 1, only the organizational capacity component is evaluated using IRS Form 990 fields such as:

- Program expenses
- Employee count
- Program service revenue
- Total expenses

The complete Capacity Gap Index will require community need indicators from Census and other public data sources in later phases.

## Proposed Sections

### 1. Introduction

- Explain why nonprofit organizational stress matters.
- Discuss the limitations of backward-looking financial assessments.
- Introduce the need for early warning and decision-support frameworks.

### 2. Literature Review

- Review nonprofit financial vulnerability research starting with Tuckman and Chang (1991).
- Discuss prior work using accounting ratios and Form 990 data.
- Identify the gap between financial vulnerability models and mission-capacity decision support.

### 3. Data

- Describe IRS Form 990 as the Phase 1 data source.
- Explain why Form 990 is useful for public nonprofit analysis.
- Discuss known limitations such as reporting lag, filing variation, missing values, and differences across Form 990, 990-EZ, and 990-PF.

### 4. Methodology

- Define the Financial Resilience Score.
- Define the organizational capacity component of the Capacity Gap Framework.
- Explain candidate indicators.
- Discuss normalization, peer comparison, and explainability.

### 5. Expected Analysis

- Apply the framework to a sample of nonprofit organizations.
- Compare organizations across revenue size, NTEE category, and geography.
- Evaluate whether multi-year patterns reveal directional stress signals.

### 6. Limitations

- IRS Form 990 data is delayed.
- Phase 1 does not include real-time internal financial data.
- Phase 1 does not include community demand data.
- The score is directional and comparative, not a definitive prediction of failure.
- Public data cannot fully capture internal operational conditions.

### 7. Future Work

- Add Census ACS and other community demand indicators.
- Develop the complete Capacity Gap Index.
- Add Mission Capacity Forecasting.
- Add Stress Signature Classification.
- Add Scenario Simulation.
- Explore explainable AI-assisted decision support.

### 8. Conclusion

- Summarize the value of public data for nonprofit early warning analysis.
- Emphasize decision support rather than deterministic prediction.
- Discuss the potential for transparent, reproducible nonprofit resilience analytics.

---

## Expected Contributions

1. A transparent and reproducible framework for assessing nonprofit financial resilience using publicly available IRS Form 990 data.

2. An original Capacity Gap Framework concept comparing community need growth against organizational capacity growth.

3. A practical early warning tool that nonprofit leaders and funders can use without access to proprietary data or expensive consulting engagements.

4. A demonstration that explainable AI approaches can be applied responsibly in the nonprofit and social sector context.

---

## Phase 1 Constraints

- IRS Form 990 data may contain reporting delays and amended filings.
- Community need indicators are not available in Phase 1, limiting the Capacity Gap Framework scope.
- The model is built on public data and may not generalize to all organizational types or sizes.
- Causal inference is not the goal of this study. The framework is designed for directional signal detection, not prediction of closure.
